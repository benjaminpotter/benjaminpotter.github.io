Pied Piper Analysis

2022-10-23

Contents

      • References

“To a musician there is only one alternative to believing that music is of divine origin, and that is to believe that nothing is.”

  • Clement Antrobus Harris

“World music … expresses the most transcendent qualities of human existence” (Bohlman, 2002). It invokes powerful emotions that feel supernatural. As such, cultures from across the globe share myths about the nature of music. Lots of stories document music as an element of the divine. In the well-known story of the Pied Piper, music has the power to enchant and capture. The myth takes place in the German town of Hamelin (Pied Piper of Hameln, n.d.). A Pied Piper offers to clear the city of its rats. Obliging, the mayor agrees to pay the Piper for his services. Satisfied, the Piper produces his magic pipe and begins to play. As he travels around the town, the rats become entranced with his music. They follow him to the river outside of town where they all drown. With the job done, the mayor refuses to pay the Piper. In return, the Piper vows to get his revenge. One Sunday, the Piper returns unannounced and begins to play his tune. This time, however, it is the town children who are bewitched. While all the townsfolk are in church, the Piper leads the children away never to be seen again. This myth deals with the idea of integrity and justice. It gives a peek into a historical period where disease and religion played a large role in everyday life. The Pied Piper also has relevant connections to today. Young people today are at the mercy of social media and often parents aren’t aware of what’s really happening online.

The Pied Piper is set in medieval Germany. It’s important to contextualize the significance of the rat in society at the time. Ratcatchers were an important part of preventing disease outbreaks. The rat was known to bring plague to households with infestations (Hardy, 2019). Specifically, mortality rates were very high among children and teenagers (Singman & Forgeng, 1999). This stressed the importance of children in the house.

Religion played an essential role in medieval German community. The church was central to social, political, and family life. The threat of divine punishment also introduced a meaningful deterrent from crime (Lynch, 2014). Specifically, the doctrine of Purgatory was responsible for maintaining order in the absence of the robust police forces of today. In the text, the question of divine intervention exists around more than just the Piper and his flute. All of the adults in the town were in church when the Piper abducted the children. God provided the means by which the Piper could carry out His punishment.

The Piper played a relative of the modern flute. In the framework of the seven elements of music, the importance of the flute is highlighted. This family of instruments has an ethereal timbre often symbolizing mystical magic. In Debussy’s Prélude à l’après-midi d’un faune, the flute plays an accompanied solo, which exemplifies its nature as a mythical instrument (Frankfurt Radio Symphony, 2014). The notion that the Piper used music to lead the rats and children speaks to its ability to enchant. Upon hearing the Piper’s flute, the children felt comfortable enough to follow him. This further identifies the Piper as a symbol of the divine and his power to wield music as a magical tool.

The myth of the Piper stays relevant in today’s world. At its core, the myth tells of a deal too good to be true, a story of deceit and justice. An excellent allegory today is the effect of social media on youth. Just as the Piper charms the children of Hamelin, the online world charms young people. Big tech is knowingly exploiting its users, especially youth (Newson et al., 2008). This is not to say that social media does not have positive aspects. It connects people across the globe. It sustained social connection over the pandemic (González-Padilla & Tortolero-Blanco, 2020). These advantages cannot go unsaid, but do not forget the Piper also cleared the town of its rat population before he captured the children. In fact, a study done for the Asian Journal of Sociology notes that most people surveyed believe that social media is negatively affecting young people (Shabir et al., 2014).

Across the world, music plays an integral role in culture and history. The human emotions that music can give rise to are exceptionally diverse and powerful. As such, people seek to document music’s divinity through myths and stories that find their way across generations. This phenomenon is unique and is at the heart of world music.
